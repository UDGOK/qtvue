questions:
  - q: "What does qtvue's 24/7 support contract include?"
    a: "Remote monitoring of every cell (telemetry, alarms, cycle-time dashboards), 24/7 hotline with median 12-minute time-to-remote-diagnosis, on-site dispatch when needed, spare-parts coverage with 48-hour delivery in 14 countries, program updates and tuning, annual safety re-validation, and a dedicated technical account manager."
  - q: "What does the support contract cost?"
    a: "Tiered: a single-cell contract runs $24K–$48K per year depending on the cell. A multi-cell line is priced per cell with a volume discount. There is no charge for the first 90 days — every qtvue cell ships with a 90-day warranty and free support window."
  - q: "What if my cell is down at 2am on a Saturday?"
    a: "Call the hotline. An on-call engineer picks up within 4 rings. We connect to your cell remotely, diagnose in under 12 minutes on median, and either walk your operator through a fix or dispatch a field tech with the right spare part. If we can't get the cell running within 4 hours, we credit that day's contract fee."
  - q: "How does remote monitoring work?"
    a: "We install a small industrial edge device on the cell's network that streams telemetry to our monitoring platform. You see the same dashboard we do — cycle time, alarm history, OEE, error codes, predictive-maintenance signals. The data stays in your region; we only see what you grant us access to."
  - q: "Where do you have spare parts?"
    a: "14 countries: US, Canada, Mexico, Brazil, UK, Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Netherlands, Poland, Czechia, Japan, South Korea. Critical wear parts (gripper jaws, vacuum cups, weld contact tips, FFC cables, encoder batteries) are stocked locally with 48-hour guaranteed delivery."
  - q: "What if the cell breaks in a country you don't have spares in?"
    a: "We ship the part from the nearest stocking hub — typical lead time is 5–7 business days. For mission-critical cells, we recommend a customer-stocked spare-parts kit (recommended spares list provided) so a part is on the shelf when you need it."
  - q: "Can my own maintenance team do first-line support?"
    a: "Yes — and we encourage it. The first 30 minutes of any cell stoppage is on your team (operator reset, E-stop check, basic I/O). If you can't resolve in 30 minutes, you call us. We provide a written troubleshooting SOP and a 4-hour training session for your maintenance staff at hand-off."
  - q: "Do you support cells you didn't build?"
    a: "Yes — about 25% of our support customers run cells originally integrated by other firms. We do a 1-week onboarding audit (review the design, the program, the safety documentation), then take over the support contract. If the existing cell has safety or quality issues, we flag them up front and quote remediation."
  - q: "What's your average uptime for supported cells?"
    a: "Across all supported cells in 2024: 99.4% uptime. Top quartile of cells (>99.7%) is the result of preventive maintenance, predictive spares replacement, and disciplined cycle-time monitoring. We publish a quarterly uptime report to every support customer."
  - q: "Can the support contract scale as we add more cells?"
    a: "Yes — add cells anytime with a 30-day notice. Pricing per cell decreases with volume. We can also add adjacent services (program tuning, vision re-calibration, EOAT refurbishment, new-product introduction) as a single bundled contract. Most customers consolidate to a single qtvue MSA after the first year."
