questions:
  - q: "What does qtvue actually do?"
    a: "qtvue sells, programs, and integrates Unitree robots for your specific use case. We are a pre-launch Unitree specialist — Go2, B2, G1, R1, H1, H2, G1-D, and arms. We work inside the same public SDKs the Unitree teams publish (unitree_sdk2, unitree_ros2, unitree_sim_isaaclab) and deliver the program, the integration, and the security posture that makes the platform do the job."
  - q: "Why Unitree?"
    a: "Unitree is shipping some of the most capable robots on the market at price points that change who's a real customer. The same platform can be a $1,600 lab purchase or a $100K industrial asset. We focus on Unitree because the software stack is consistent across the range, the SDKs are public, and the price points make our engagement model work."
  - q: "Who is qtvue's typical customer?"
    a: "Four buyer types, all mixed: (1) manufacturers automating a process they can't easily automate any other way, (2) manufacturing engineers who need a programming partner for a platform they already own, (3) research and education labs doing legged-robotics or manipulation work, (4) security and inspection teams looking for autonomous patrol. The engagement is shaped to the use case, not the other way around."
  - q: "Where is qtvue based, and where do you operate?"
    a: "Engineering and integration is led from the United States. On-site work is performed where the customer is — we have a partner network for regions we don't staff directly. Programming and remote work happens anywhere; integration requires physical presence."
  - q: "How long does a typical engagement take?"
    a: "Programming-only engagements (you already own the platform): 2–8 weeks. Sell-and-Program (we supply the platform and write the code): 4–10 weeks. Full integration (mounting, networking, perception, training): 6–16 weeks. We give you a realistic timeline in the engineering review, not a sales date."
  - q: "Do you build custom robots, or integrate off-the-shelf Unitree?"
    a: "We integrate off-the-shelf Unitree. We do not manufacture robots. We do build custom EOAT (end-of-arm tooling), custom mounts, and custom perception stacks. This keeps your hardware supportable through Unitree's normal channels and your spares standardized."
  - q: "What is the cost of a qtvue engagement?"
    a: "Programming-only: $5K–$60K. Sell-and-Program with a Go2 or R1: $15K–$80K. Full integration of a B2 or G1 with custom EOAT: $80K–$300K. Pricing is fixed, written, and approved before we start work. We don't do time-and-materials."
  - q: "How do I get started?"
    a: "Submit your use case via the intake form. Five questions, three minutes. We reply with an engineering review within 1 business day. The review is free, names the right platform, the SDK plan, the rough timeline, and a no-surprise cost band."
  - q: "Do you offer ongoing support after the engagement?"
    a: "Yes. We offer 30-day on-site warranty after sign-off as standard, and optional long-term support contracts for fleets. We don't do 24/7 hotlines pre-launch — if you need a support contract, we'll quote it case by case."
  - q: "Are you hiring?"
    a: "We're growing the engineering team selectively. See /careers for open roles. We hire robotics engineers with real Unitree, ROS 2, and Isaac Lab experience — not just robotics engineers generically."
