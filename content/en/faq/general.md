questions:
  - q: "What does qtvue actually do?"
    a: "qtvue sells, programs, and integrates industrial robots end-to-end. We design the cell in 3D, simulate cycle time, source the hardware, write the program (FANUC KAREL, ABB RAPID, KUKA KRL, Yaskawa, UR, etc.), integrate it on your floor, and provide 24/7 support. From first feasibility call to first good part, we own the whole project."
  - q: "Who is qtvue's typical customer?"
    a: "Mid-market manufacturers and ambitious operators who want a turnkey robotic cell without hiring a full in-house automation team. We work with 240+ operations teams across welding, material handling, packaging, machine tending, and assembly. Typical deal size is $150K–$1.2M."
  - q: "Where is qtvue based, and where do you operate?"
    a: "We are headquartered in the United States and operate globally with remote hand-off and on-site service in 14 countries. Our engineering is done in-house; field integration is performed by qtvue-employed technicians, never subcontractors."
  - q: "How long does a typical project take from first call to first part?"
    a: "Most cells go from kickoff to first good part in 6 weeks, with cell commissioning in under 6 weeks for standard integrations. Complex multi-robot lines with custom EOAT run 10–14 weeks. We book projects on a Q-by-Q basis."
  - q: "Do you build custom robots, or integrate off-the-shelf arms?"
    a: "We integrate off-the-shelf industrial arms from FANUC, ABB, KUKA, Yaskawa/Motoman, and Universal Robots. We do not manufacture robots — we design the cell, write the program, build the end-of-arm tooling (often custom), and own the integration. This keeps your hardware supportable through the OEM and your spares standardized."
  - q: "What is the cost of a qtvue cell?"
    a: "A single-robot cell with standard EOAT typically runs $180K–$450K fully installed. Pricing is a function of payload, reach, EOAT complexity, sensors/vision, and safety scope. We provide a fixed-price quote after a 30-minute scoping call, no commitment. Larger multi-robot lines are quoted per-cell."
  - q: "Do you take equity, barter, or non-cash compensation?"
    a: "No. We are a focused services business; every project is a fixed-price cash engagement with a clear SOW and acceptance criteria. If your budget is uncertain, our process is to scope a smaller pilot cell first."
  - q: "How do I get started?"
    a: "Book a 30-minute scoping call via the contact page. We respond within 1 business day. The call is free, no commitment, and you'll leave with a written scope summary whether or not you move forward with us."
  - q: "Do you offer ongoing support after the cell ships?"
    a: "Yes. Every qtvue cell ships with a 90-day warranty and an optional 24/7 support contract covering remote monitoring, on-site service, spare parts, and program updates. Spare parts are stocked in 14 countries with guaranteed 48-hour delivery on critical parts."
  - q: "Are you hiring?"
    a: "Yes — we are growing the engineering and field integration teams. See /careers for open roles. We hire robotics engineers, integration technicians, vision specialists, and sales engineers."
