  - q: "When was qtvue founded?"
    a: "qtvue was founded in 2021. The company was built by robotics engineers from FANUC and ABB who wanted to deliver turnkey industrial automation to mid-market manufacturers without the legacy integrator overhead."
  - q: "How big is qtvue?"
    a: "We are a focused team of 38 people as of 2026: 22 robotics and vision engineers, 8 field integration technicians, 4 account and program managers, and 4 operations and finance. We plan to grow to ~60 by end of 2026 and are actively hiring (see /careers)."
  - q: "Where is qtvue based?"
    a: "Engineering headquarters in the United States. We have field integration teams deployed in 14 countries and a network of regional partners for the long tail. Remote work is standard for engineering; field work is on-site at customer facilities."
  - q: "What certifications does qtvue hold?"
    a: "ISO 9001:2015 (quality management). FANUC Certified Integrator. ABB Authorized Value Provider. KUKA System Partner. Yaskawa Motoman Partner. Universal Robots Certified Integrator. CE marking capability for European deployments. ITAR and NIST 800-171 registered for aerospace/defense work."
  - q: "What's qtvue's mission?"
    a: "Make turnkey industrial robotics accessible to mid-market manufacturers. The historical barrier to automation was integrator overhead, long timelines, and proprietary lock-in. We are the antidote: fixed-price, fast, open-source-friendly, with your team in the loop at every step."
  - q: "How is qtvue different from a traditional system integrator?"
    a: "Three differences. First, fixed-price projects with milestone payments — not time-and-materials. Second, code-as-deliverable — your team gets the program source in a git repo, not a black box. Third, our average cell goes from kickoff to first part in 6 weeks vs the industry standard 16–24 weeks."
  - q: "Does qtvue have a published uptime SLA?"
    a: "Yes — on every 24/7 support contract. The SLA is 99% uptime for standard cells and 99.5% for validated cells. We credit the contract fee for any month we miss the SLA. Across all supported cells in 2024 we averaged 99.4% uptime."
  - q: "Who are qtvue's investors?"
    a: "qtvue is privately held and profitable. We have raised a small seed round from operator-friendly angels (manufacturing CEOs, automation veterans). We have no outside strategic investors and no exit pressure — we are building for the long term."
  - q: "What is qtvue's stance on proprietary lock-in?"
    a: "Anti-lock-in, in writing. You own every line of program code, every design file, every document we produce for your project. You can take it to another integrator tomorrow. We earn your repeat business on performance, not on captive customers."
  - q: "How do I follow qtvue's work?"
    a: "Subscribe to our [engineering blog](/blog), follow us on LinkedIn, or book a 30-minute scoping call to see what we are working on. We publish a quarterly customer letter and an annual impact report."
