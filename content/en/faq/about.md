  - q: "When was qtvue founded and what's your stage?"
    a: "qtvue is a pre-launch company — we have paying customers and shipped engagements, but we don't have a 240-cell case-study library and we won't fake one. We are building credibility through founder-led technical content, process transparency, and a small number of careful engagements."
  - q: "What size is the team?"
    a: "We are a focused team. Engineering is the bulk of the headcount; we don't carry sales or marketing overhead. We will grow, but only at the rate the engagements demand — we won't pre-emptively scale ahead of real work."
  - q: "Do you have a published uptime SLA?"
    a: "Not yet. We will, once we have enough live engagements to compute it honestly. We won't publish a number we can't back."
  - q: "What certifications does qtvue hold?"
    a: "We are a Unitree specialist. We hold the standard partner-level engagement status. We do not currently pursue ISO 9001 or other broad certifications because pre-launch they would be a vanity spend, not a real signal. We will add them when the customer base demands them."
  - q: "What's qtvue's mission?"
    a: "Make the Unitree range actually do useful work in the real world. The platform is capable; the gap is in the program, the integration, and the security posture. We close that gap."
  - q: "How is qtvue different from a Unitree reseller?"
    a: "Resellers ship the box. We make the box do the job. The difference is in the program, the integration, and the security work — which is what most Unitree customers actually need help with."
  - q: "Who are qtvue's investors?"
    a: "We are privately held and capital-efficient. We do not have outside strategic investors, and we are not optimizing for a quick exit. We are building for the long term."
  - q: "What is qtvue's stance on proprietary lock-in?"
    a: "Anti-lock-in, in writing. You own every line of program code, every design file, every document we produce for your project. You can take it to another integrator tomorrow. We earn your repeat business on performance, not on captive customers."
  - q: "How do I follow qtvue's work?"
    a: "The [engineering blog](/blog) is the source of truth. We publish founder-written technical articles about the Unitree stack — programming the G1, deploying the Go2, sim-to-real in Isaac Lab. The blog is the most current view of what we know and what we're learning."
  - q: "What does qtvue do that it won't talk about publicly?"
    a: "Some of our engagement work is under NDA. We do not publish customer details, customer names, or specifics of in-progress work without explicit permission. The blog is what we choose to publish; everything else is the customer's."
