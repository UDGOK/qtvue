questions:
  - q: "What's the difference between qtvue's services?"
    a: "Six core services: Cell Design (3D layout, simulation, cycle-time study), Robot Programming (FANUC KAREL, ABB RAPID, KUKA KRL, etc.), Vision & Sensing (2D/3D vision, force/torque, conveyor tracking), End-of-Arm Tooling (grippers, weld guns, custom), Safety & Compliance (ISO 10218, ISO/TS 15066, risk assessments), and 24/7 Support (remote monitoring, on-site service, spare parts). You can buy any one standalone or bundle them."
  - q: "Which service should I start with?"
    a: "Always start with Cell Design. Even if you have a working robot, the design phase de-risks the rest of the project by validating reach, cycle time, and safety scope before fabrication. A paid design study is the cheapest insurance you can buy — about 1 in 12 results in a 'don't build this' recommendation that saves you six figures."
  - q: "Do I have to use all six services?"
    a: "No — most projects use 3–5 of the six. A typical welding cell might be Cell Design + Programming + EOAT + Safety, with Vision optional and 24/7 Support added after hand-off. A high-mix bin-picking cell might be Cell Design + Programming + Vision + EOAT + Safety, with 24/7 Support as standard."
  - q: "Can I buy a single phase of the project?"
    a: "Yes. You can engage us for a standalone design study, a rescue-programming job, a safety re-assessment, or a one-week cycle-time optimization. Most clients start with a single phase and expand as the relationship develops."
  - q: "How do you handle ongoing work for an existing customer?"
    a: "About 70% of our annual revenue comes from existing customers. We have a standing engineering team allocated to each active customer, a quarterly review cadence, and an MSA that lets you issue task orders against a pre-negotiated rate card."
  - q: "Do you provide training for our team?"
    a: "Yes — operator training, maintenance training, and programmer training are all standard deliverables on every project. Operator: 2-hour hands-on. Maintenance: 4-hour technical. Programmer: 2-day deep dive including how to modify the program for new SKUs."
  - q: "What's the typical project timeline?"
    a: "6 weeks for a standard cell, 10–14 weeks for a complex multi-robot line. The 6-week timeline is: Week 1 design study, Week 2 hardware procurement, Weeks 3-4 cell build and programming, Week 5 FAT (factory acceptance test), Week 6 SAT (site acceptance test) and hand-off."
  - q: "What about international projects?"
    a: "About 30% of our projects are non-US. We handle logistics, customs, and on-site integration in 14 countries. For projects outside those 14, we partner with a local integration firm for the on-site work and own the engineering and remote support."
  - q: "Do you sell hardware or just integration services?"
    a: "We are a services-led business. We specify the hardware, source it through the OEM channels (so you get OEM warranty and support), and bundle the cost into our fixed-price quote. You own the hardware, the program source, the documentation, and the warranty — we never lock you in."
  - q: "How do I get a quote?"
    a: "Book a 30-minute scoping call via the contact page. We respond within 1 business day. The call is free and you'll get a written scope summary whether or not you move forward. For larger projects, we issue a paid design study (fixed price) before quoting the integration."
