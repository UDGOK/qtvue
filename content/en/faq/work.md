questions:
  - q: "Why don't you publish more case studies?"
    a: "About 60% of our customers prefer to stay anonymous due to competitive sensitivity. We publish only the projects where the customer has approved a public case study with named metrics. If you want a deep case study on a specific application, ask — we may have a non-public one to share under NDA."
  - q: "How do I see the cell before I commit?"
    a: "We host customer visits at our integration facility for any qualified project. You can also request a recorded walkthrough video of a similar cell in operation. For larger projects, we arrange a reference call with an existing customer in a similar industry."
  - q: "Can I talk to a reference customer?"
    a: "Yes — for any project over $300K we provide a reference call with a comparable customer (similar industry, similar cell scope). The reference can speak to timeline, cost, uptime, and how we handled issues. We anonymize the reference at the customer's request if needed."
  - q: "What metrics do your case studies show?"
    a: "Three numbers: throughput (% improvement vs the manual baseline), payback (months to recover the project cost from labor savings), and uptime (for supported cells). These are the three metrics our customers care about. We don't publish vanity metrics (square footage, headcount, etc.)."
  - q: "What if my application isn't in the case studies?"
    a: "Most of our work isn't in the case studies — we publish roughly the top 20% by impact, the rest is under NDA. If your application isn't represented, ask us directly. The odds are good we've done it, or something close enough to extrapolate from."
  - q: "Do you work with competitors of your existing customers?"
    a: "We have non-disclosure agreements that include non-compete clauses for some customers. In general, we will not work on a project for a direct competitor of an existing customer in the same product line. We can usually find a creative workaround (different team, different scope) when needed."
  - q: "How long does it take to see ROI on a qtvue cell?"
    a: "Median payback across our published case studies: 14 months. The range is wide: 6 months for a high-volume dedicated cell replacing 3+ operators, 30+ months for a flexible cell replacing 1 operator. The scoping call includes a ROI estimate so you know the payback range before committing."
  - q: "What's your largest cell to date?"
    a: "A 12-robot automotive body framing line for a tier-1 supplier. The cell includes 6 weld robots, 4 material-handling robots, 2 vision-guided sealant robots, and a coordinated safety scope with 24 safety zones. Total project value: $4.2M, 14-month engagement."
  - q: "What's the smallest project you've taken?"
    a: "A single UR5 cobot in a small machine shop, $42K all-in, 2-week timeline. We will absolutely do small projects — they are how we build long-term customer relationships. About 25% of our annual revenue comes from projects under $100K."
  - q: "Can I see a sample SOW or proposal before I commit to a scoping call?"
    a: "Not publicly — every SOW is tailored to the project. We can share a redacted SOW from a comparable engagement under NDA so you can see our level of detail and the contractual structure. Ask during the scoping call."
