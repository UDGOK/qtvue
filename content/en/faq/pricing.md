questions:
  - q: "How much does a qtvue cell cost?"
    a: "A single-robot cell with standard EOAT typically runs $180K–$450K fully installed. Pricing is a function of payload, reach, EOAT complexity, sensors/vision, and safety scope. Multi-robot lines scale linearly: a 4-robot palletizing line is typically $700K–$1.4M. We provide a fixed-price quote after a 30-minute scoping call."
  - q: "What's included in the price?"
    a: "Everything: the design study, the hardware (robot, EOAT, vision, safety, fencing, controls), the integration labor, the on-site commissioning, the operator and maintenance training, the program source code in a private git repo, the safety documentation, and the 90-day warranty. You won't get change orders for 'extras' — the price is the price."
  - q: "What's NOT included in the price?"
    a: "Facility preparation (concrete, electrical service, compressed air, network drops) is on you. So is shipping if you're outside our 14 supported countries. Annual support contracts are quoted separately. Anything not in the written SOW is not in the price — we don't do 'while we're in there'."
  - q: "What are the payment terms?"
    a: "Standard: 30% on order, 30% on FAT (factory acceptance test), 30% on SAT (site acceptance test), 10% on 90-day warranty expiry. For larger projects we can structure progress payments against milestones. We don't do net-60+ on first projects."
  - q: "Can I pay in installments?"
    a: "Yes — the milestone-based structure above is effectively installments. We can also lease the cell through our financing partner (3- and 5-year terms, fixed monthly payments, with the option to own the hardware at end of term)."
  - q: "Is there a discount for multiple cells?"
    a: "Yes — about 8–15% off list for a second cell, scaling up for a line. We also offer a 'fleet' discount for customers running 4+ cells. The discount is on the integration labor, not the hardware (hardware is OEM-priced)."
  - q: "What about R&D or pilot projects?"
    a: "We do paid pilot engagements. A 4-week pilot cell is typically $50K–$90K and results in a working cell on your floor with a written recommendation for the full-scale rollout. About 80% of our pilots convert to full-scale projects."
  - q: "How does pricing compare to in-house automation?"
    a: "In-house automation is cheaper in the second year and much more expensive in the first. The build-vs-buy math depends on how many cells you're commissioning per year and how much of the engineering capacity you have on staff. We have a free build-vs-buy calculator we share during the scoping call."
  - q: "Do you accept purchase orders, or do I need a credit application?"
    a: "Both. First-time customers go through a short credit application (typically <24 hours to clear). After that, POs are accepted against a signed MSA. We accept wire transfer, ACH, and (for US customers) check."
  - q: "What if I need to cancel a project mid-flight?"
    a: "We invoice for work performed to date plus a 15% cancellation fee on the remaining scope. You own all work product (designs, programs, documentation) generated to that point, and we hand it over in a clean, documented state."
