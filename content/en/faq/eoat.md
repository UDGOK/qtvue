questions:
  - q: "What kinds of EOAT does qtvue design and build?"
    a: "Pneumatic and servo-electric grippers (Schunk, OnRobot, Destaco, Piab), vacuum grippers (Piab, Schmalz, COVAL), weld guns (Miller, Lincoln, Fronius, Tregaskiss), dispense heads (Nordson, Dymax, Graco), nutrunners, riveters, and fully custom EOAT for unique parts. About 30% of our EOAT work is fully custom."
  - q: "When do you use pneumatic vs electric grippers?"
    a: "Pneumatic: cheaper, faster, simpler — right for high-volume dedicated cells where the part never changes. Electric: flexible, positionable, force-controllable — right for high-mix cells where the same gripper handles many SKUs, or when you need to control grip force precisely (fragile parts, assembly)."
  - q: "How long does a custom EOAT take?"
    a: "Design: 1–2 weeks. Fabrication: 2–4 weeks. Integration and tuning: 1 week. Total: 4–7 weeks for a fully custom tool. We always try to use catalog grippers first — 70% of 'custom' engagements end up using a standard gripper with custom fingers, which is faster and cheaper."
  - q: "Can you change tooling for high-mix production?"
    a: "Yes. We design multi-tool changers (manual, automatic, or tool-rack systems) that let a single robot switch between grippers in 5–30 seconds. We integrate with the most common changer brands (Stäubli, Schunk, ATI) and design the magazine layout to fit your cell."
  - q: "What about compliance and force control in the EOAT?"
    a: "We integrate compliance devices (passive RCCs, servo compliance) for insertion and assembly tasks where part-to-part variation is large. For force-controlled applications, we put the F/T sensor between the robot flange and the gripper, with a clean cable management plan."
  - q: "Do you size vacuum grippers for our parts?"
    a: "Yes. We calculate the required holding force from the part weight, the vacuum level, the seal area, and a 2× safety factor. We specify the right vacuum generator (venturi or electric pump), the cups or foam, and the blow-off logic. We also handle the EOAT-side vacuum reservoir for parts that need a moment of additional grip during fast moves."
  - q: "What about welding EOAT specifically?"
    a: "We build and tune weld guns for MIG, TIG, and spot welding. We size the transformer, choose the right Tregaskiss or Fronius torch, route the wire feed, integrate the dress pack, and tune the robot's weave pattern. We work with the weld engineer on your team to develop the weld procedure spec (WPS) and qualify the first article."
  - q: "Can you handle the EOAT side of dispense applications?"
    a: "Yes. We integrate Nordson, Dymax, and Graco dispense heads for adhesives, sealants, mastics, and UV-cure materials. The EOAT includes the dispense valve, the heated hose (for hot-melt), the needle or nozzle, the position servo, and the clean-shutoff logic."
  - q: "What's the cost of custom EOAT?"
    a: "Custom fingers on a catalog gripper: $2K–$8K. Fully custom pneumatic gripper: $8K–$25K. Custom weld gun: $15K–$60K including the transformer. Complex multi-tool changers: $40K–$150K. We always quote fixed-price after the design phase."
  - q: "What about maintenance and spare parts for EOAT?"
    a: "We document every EOAT in a maintenance manual: parts list, lubrication schedule, expected wear items, and recommended spares. Critical wear parts (gripper jaws, vacuum cups, weld contact tips) ship with the cell in a labeled spare-parts kit. Restock is a 48-hour delivery in 14 countries."
