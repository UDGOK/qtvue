questions:
  - q: "Which industries does qtvue serve?"
    a: "Automotive (tier 1 and tier 2 suppliers, EV battery assembly), aerospace (engine components, structural parts), medical devices (disposables, instruments, implants), food & beverage (primary packaging, palletizing), consumer goods (personal care, household products), logistics (order fulfillment, sortation), and general manufacturing (welding, machine tending, assembly)."
  - q: "Do you work in regulated industries (FDA, medical)?"
    a: "Yes — we work in FDA-regulated medical device manufacturing. Our medical work includes validated process documentation, IQ/OQ/PQ support, and design controls aligned with 21 CFR 820. We can quote under a quality agreement with full traceability and a dedicated quality contact."
  - q: "What about food-grade and washdown environments?"
    a: "We build cells rated to IP65 / IP67 / IP69K for food and beverage, with stainless and food-grade components, hygienic cable routing, and washdown-rated sensors and grippers. We have experience in dairy, baked goods, ready-meal, beverage, and fresh-produce environments."
  - q: "How do you handle high-mix / low-volume production?"
    a: "High-mix is one of our specialties. We design cells with quick-change tooling, recipe-driven program selection, and vision-guided adaptation so a single cell can run 50+ SKUs in a shift. We have customers running 300+ SKUs through a single cell with <2% changeover overhead."
  - q: "What about high-volume / dedicated production?"
    a: "We design for cycle time and uptime, not flexibility. Typical high-volume cells run 5–15 second cycles with 99%+ uptime. We use servo-driven motion, predictive maintenance, and redundant critical systems (dual encoders, backup vacuum generators) to maximize OEE."
  - q: "Do you do logistics and warehouse robotics?"
    a: "Yes — but specifically the fixed-arm, high-throughput kind (palletizing, depalletizing, singulation, induction to sorter, decanting from totes). We do not do AMRs / mobile robots. We work well alongside AMR fleets from Locus, 6 River Systems, Symbotic, AutoStore, and similar."
  - q: "What about aerospace and defense?"
    a: "We have an aerospace practice focused on drilling, fastening, sealing, and layup for engine and structural components. We work with primes and tier-1 suppliers under ITAR and NIST 800-171 controls. Most aerospace cells are validated against customer-specific process specs (BAC, BMS, etc.)."
  - q: "Do you do EV battery assembly?"
    a: "Yes — cell stacking, module assembly, pack assembly, busbar welding, and end-of-line testing for EV batteries. We work with pouch, cylindrical, and prismatic form factors. Most of this work is in dry rooms, ATEX-rated zones, or both."
  - q: "What about consumer goods and personal care?"
    a: "High-speed primary packaging, cartoning, case packing, and palletizing for personal care, household products, and OTC. We work in GMP-grade environments when the product requires it. Cycle times in the 1–3 second range are typical."
  - q: "What if my industry isn't on this list?"
    a: "Ask us. We've done one-off cells for marine, agriculture, energy, and mining — applications that don't fit a clean industry category but share the same engineering challenges. If we can't help, we'll point you to a partner who can."
